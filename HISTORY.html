The life and times of Mandela

The Early years

Nelson Mandela, a global symbol of freedom and resilience, sacrificed much in the fight against apartheid, inspiring generations with his dedication to justice and reconciliation.

The political journey

Born in 1918, Nelson Mandela was shaped by Thembu royal influence. After studying at Fort Hare, he joined the ANC, co-founding its Youth League in 1944 to lead a stronger fight against apartheid

The prison years

In the 1950s, Mandela led the Defiance Campaign, co-founded South Africa's first Black law firm, and developed the M-Plan to support underground ANC activities amid increasing arrests and restrictions.
